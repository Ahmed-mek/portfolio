# Electric Vehicle Motor Driver (4.5 kW / 72V) — Teknofest EV Competition

**Category:** Hardware & PCB · Embedded Control  
**Status:** ✅ Completed (2024 – 2025)  
**Type:** International Competition — International Efficiency Challenge Electric Vehicle Races (Teknofest Finalist 2024 & 2025)

---

## Overview

A high-power BLDC motor driver engineered for a **4.5 kW, 72V electric vehicle competition platform** — designed to deliver competitive efficiency, reliability, and performance under the demanding conditions of the International Efficiency Challenge EV Races (Elektromobil category). The team achieved **Teknofest Finalist** status in both 2024 and 2025.

Beyond the motor driver, the project encompassed a 3 kW power supply, BMS research support, and a local SMPS-based charging unit — covering the full EV powertrain support chain.

---

## Key Contributions

### 4.5 kW BLDC Motor Driver
- Designed a **72V / 4.5 kW 3-phase BLDC motor driver** for the competition EV, including MOSFET H-bridge selection, gate driver circuitry, current sensing, and thermal management (heatsink + cooling fan).
- Implemented protection mechanisms: overcurrent, overvoltage, overtemperature, and phase-loss detection for safe high-power operation.
- Integrated **CAN bus interface** for communication with the vehicle management system and BMS.

### 3 kW Power Supply
- Designed a **3 kW power supply** based on a 220V-to-72V AC transformer for induction-load inspection and bench testing of the EV system prior to vehicle integration.

### BMS Research & Development
- Researched and contributed to the **Battery Management System (BMS)** development, supporting cell monitoring, protection logic, and state estimation for the vehicle's energy storage system.
- Contributed to CAN-based BMS communication protocol design.

### SMPS Charging Unit
- Contributed to the design of a **local onboard charging unit** applying SMPS topologies (flyback / LLC) and power-conversion principles to enable AC mains charging of the EV battery pack.

### Team Leadership
- Coordinated project delivery, technical execution, and team performance under strict competition deadlines across both 2024 and 2025 competition cycles.
